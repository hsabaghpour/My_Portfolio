Support_Vector_Machine

Exploring Support Vector Machines (SVMs) with the Iris dataset! SVMs shine in classification, regression, and outlier detection, particularly with smaller datasets like Iris. The Linear SVM method seeks the widest street (large margin) to separate classes using support vectors, emphasizing the importance of scaled features. Soft Margin, on the other hand, offers flexibility by balancing margin width and violations, controlled by the CCC parameter. Image Classifier with Keras


In this project, we'll use Keras and the Sequential API to build an image classifier for the Fashion MNIST dataset. Unlike the simpler MNIST dataset of handwritten digits, Fashion MNIST consists of 70,000 grayscale images (28 × 28 pixels) of various fashion items, spread across 10 classes. Despite its similarity in format to MNIST, Fashion MNIST is more challenging, with simple models achieving only around 83% accuracy compared to MNIST's 92%. Our goal is to load this dataset, build and train the classifier using Keras, and demonstrate its ability to handle complex image classification tasks effectively.
